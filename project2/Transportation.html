CGR is contributing substantially to India's thrust to develop & upgrade the surface transport network at par with international standards. Commencing on a
steady note, the Company has garnered experience in executing National Highways, State Highways for MoRTH, NHAI, State PWD including World Bank, Asian
Development Bank, JICA & JNNURM funded projects (under EPC and BOT formats).
CGR has experience in execution of speciality roads such as BRTS Corridors with allied structures such as Fly Overs, ROBs, and Grade Separators etc.
and has also delivered key Urban Road Projects in cities surmounting challenges such as traffic congestions, diversions, coordination with multiple public utilities
and concerned agencies. Some such projects were executed in Hyderabad, Visakhapatnam, Trichy, Jamshedpur and Ranchi, meeting the challenge of stiff time
schedules.

Featured Project

Transportation- Roads

Up-gradation of Tanda-Raebareli Section to 2- lane with paved shoulders in the State of Uttar Pradesh on EPC mode

Upgradation to two lanes with paved shoulders of existing NH-232 and NH-330 from Tanda to Raebareli with total length of 155.90 km in the state of Uttar
Pradesh on EPC (Engineering, Procurement and Construction) mode under National Highway Development Program.
The scope of work includes Widening and Strengthening of existing road to a 2-lane Carriageway as per MORTH standards. The Project also includes
construction of new structures such as ROBs, Major Bridges, Vehicular Under-Passes and other components in its Scope.

Four Laning of Hyderabad-Yadgiri section of NH-202 on DBFOT Toll Basis

Widening and Strengthening of the existing road from Km 18+600 to 54+000 of NH-202 in the state of Telangana into a 4-lane Dual Carriageway separated by
Central Median as per MORTH standards. Scope of the Project also includes construction of new structures such as ROBs, Major Bridges, Grade Separators,
Vehicular & Cattle Underpasses and other structures.
The project was awarded on Design, Build, Finance, Operate and Transfer (DBFOT) Toll basis under National Highway Development Programme of the
Government of India and is in commercial operation.

Development of SH-23 Ginigere-Gangavathi-Sindhanur section

Widening and Strengthening of existing road of Ginigere-Gangavathi-Sindhanur of SH-23 from Km 79+00 to 161+99 in the state of Karnataka into a 2-Lane
Carriageway as per MORTH standards. The Project also includes construction of new structures such as Major Bridges, Minor Bridges and other components in
its Scope.
The Project was awarded through PPP mode on DBFOT (Design, Build, Finance, Operate and Transfer) Toll basis by KRDCL and is in commercial operation.
